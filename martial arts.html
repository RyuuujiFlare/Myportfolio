All of this are my martial arts

Martial Art Description Strengths Weaknesses
Kickboxing Kickboxing is a dynamic martial art that combines elements of traditional boxing with various kicking techniques. It originated in Japan in the 1960s and has since become popular worldwide for its effective striking techniques and intense workouts. Kickboxing is not only a martial art but also a competitive sport and a fitness regimen. High-intensity workouts, effective striking techniques. Limited ground fighting skills.
Boxing Boxing, also known as the "Sweet Science," emphasizes the use of precise footwork, head movement, and powerful punches. It's not just a sport but also a discipline that hones mental focus and physical conditioning. Exceptional punching techniques, footwork. Lacks grappling and kicking techniques.
Capoeira Capoeira is a Brazilian martial art disguised as dance and acrobatics. Its roots trace back to African slaves in Brazil who used it for self-defense. Capoeira's fluid movements and music make it a captivating and culturally rich martial art. Fluid movements, acrobatics, cultural significance. Limited effectiveness in direct combat situations.
Aikido Aikido is often referred to as the "art of peace." It focuses on using an opponent's energy against them, rather than directly opposing force. Aikido techniques include throws, joint locks, and pins, making it an effective martial art for self-defense. Redirecting opponent's energy, joint locks. Relies heavily on cooperation from the opponent.
JJJ (Jiu-Jitsu) Jiu-Jitsu, meaning "gentle art," is a grappling-based martial art that focuses on controlling opponents through techniques such as joint locks and chokeholds. It emphasizes leverage and technique over strength, making it suitable for practitioners of all sizes. Ground fighting, submissions, self-defense. Vulnerable to strikes in stand-up situations.
Muay Thai Muay Thai, also known as the "Art of Eight Limbs," utilizes punches, kicks, elbows, and knee strikes. It originated in Thailand and is known for its raw power and effectiveness in both self-defense and sport combat. Powerful strikes using fists, elbows, knees, and shins. Limited grappling techniques.
Taekwondo (TKD) Taekwondo is a Korean martial art known for its high kicks and fast-paced techniques. It places a strong emphasis on kicking techniques, but also includes hand strikes, blocks, and forms (poomsae). High kicks, fast and dynamic movements. Focuses primarily on kicks, lacks hand techniques.
Karate Karate, meaning "empty hand," originated in Okinawa and later spread to Japan and beyond. It emphasizes powerful strikes, blocks, and stances. Karate practitioners often focus on self-discipline and personal development. Powerful strikes, forms (katas). Limited grappling and ground fighting.
Kali-Arnis Kali-Arnis, also known as Eskrima or Filipino Martial Arts (FMA), focuses on weapons-based fighting. Practitioners learn to use sticks, knives, and other improvised weapons, as well as empty-hand techniques. It's a practical martial art with roots in Filipino culture. Weapons training, fluid movements, coordination. Requires access to weapons, less effective in unarmed combat.
Systema Systema, originating from Russia, is a martial art that focuses on adaptability and efficiency in combat. It incorporates elements of striking, grappling, and weapon handling, with an emphasis on breath control and relaxation under stress. Breath control, relaxation under stress, practical self-defense. Lack of standardized techniques, varying quality of instruction.
